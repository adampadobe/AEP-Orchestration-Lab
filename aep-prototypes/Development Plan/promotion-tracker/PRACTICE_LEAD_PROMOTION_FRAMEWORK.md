# Practice Lead Promotion Framework

This document maps the **Practice Lead** levelling criteria to the activities you track and helps you assess gaps.

---

## The eight Practice Lead competencies

(From SC IC Levelling Guide – Practice Lead level.)

1. **SCOPE OF RESPONSIBILITY** – SME; go-to for a problem/challenge/deal type; reputation in BU for influencing and closing big deals.  
2. **INFLUENCE** – Owns day-to-day client communication; trusted advisor to all levels.  
3. **STRATEGIC DIRECTION** – Credibility in leading meetings; drives conversations across broad network; PM ownership for own focus area.  
4. **FUNCTIONAL KNOWLEDGE** – Leads complex deals, multi-solution, atypical requirements; discovery and scoping; coaches others; escalates to leadership.  
5. **CUSTOMER ENGAGEMENT** – Owns client communication; leads discussions with/without prep; ad-hoc and future-focused questions; knows when to probe or challenge.  
6. **PROJECT MANAGEMENT SKILLS** – Supports 1–2 team members to effectively project manage their deals.  
7. **TEAM CONTRIBUTION** – Leading, mentoring, managing projects and team; shapes peers; Summit sessions, quarterly blog or webinars.  
8. **PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT** – Leads enablement; dedicated mentor to 1–2; mentors 1–2 on presentation skills.

---

## What evidence to collect (by competency)

| Competency | What to collect | Activity types that fit |
|------------|------------------|--------------------------|
| **Scope of responsibility** | SME examples; deals you influenced/closed; reputation stories. | Deals, strategic customer, innovation (SME proof). |
| **Influence** | Client communication ownership; trusted-advisor examples; levels you work with. | Strategic customer, mentoring (internal influence). |
| **Strategic direction** | Meetings you led; cross-functional/network reach; PM ownership. | Product sync, strategic customer, community. |
| **Functional knowledge** | Complex deals; discovery/scoping; coaching others; escalation. | Deals, strategic customer, innovation, mentoring. |
| **Customer engagement** | Leading discussions; ad-hoc/future questions; probing/challenging. | Strategic customer, deals. |
| **Project management skills** | Helping 1–2 people PM their deals; examples of support. | Mentoring (with purpose/impact stating PM support). |
| **Team contribution** | Leading/mentoring projects and team; Summit/blog/webinar. | Community, mentoring, innovation (team adoption). |
| **Professional development** | Enablement sessions; dedicated mentor 1–2; presentation mentoring. | Mentoring, community, certifications. |

---

## How to assess your progress

Use the dashboard and exports to answer:

1. **Coverage** – Do you have at least 1–2 strong examples per competency? (Check “By competency category” on Overview.)  
2. **Mentoring** – Are you at “dedicated mentor to 1–2” and “mentors 1–2 on presentation skills”? (Unique mentees, sustained relationships, hours.)  
3. **Scale** – Can you cite numbers? (Sessions, hours, adoption count, pipeline/revenue.)  
4. **Sustained commitment** – Any 2+ session relationships? (Dashboard: “Sustained relationships.”)  
5. **External presence** – Summit, blog, or webinar in the tracker? (Community tab.)

---

## Recommended actions when you see gaps

| Gap | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Weak on **Scope** | Log more deals and strategic customer work; add evidence of SME and deal influence. |
| Weak on **Influence** | Log strategic customer engagements with role/level; add mentoring that shows internal influence. |
| Weak on **Strategic direction** | Add product/engineering syncs; community or cross-team initiatives. |
| Weak on **Functional knowledge** | Add complex deal support, discovery work, and innovations with adoption. |
| Weak on **Customer engagement** | Log strategic customer and deals with “evidence” describing how you lead and probe. |
| Weak on **Project management** | Add mentoring entries where purpose/impact = supporting them to PM their deals. |
| Weak on **Team contribution** | Add community (workshops, Summit, blog, webinar); link innovations to team adoption. |
| Weak on **Professional development** | Add enablement (community); ensure 1–2 dedicated mentees and presentation mentoring (mentoring entries). |

---

## Using the tracker to build your case

- **Evidence field** – Write 1–2 sentences: “This demonstrates Practice Lead by …”  
- **Impact field** – Quantify where possible: hours, people, pipeline, adoption.  
- **Category** – Tag every entry with the best-fit competency so the dashboard shows balance.  
- **Export** – Use “Combined + Export” to generate CSV/JSON for your promotion package.

After 3–6 months you should be able to say: “Here’s the data” – totals, unique people, hours, sustained relationships, and evidence mapped to each Practice Lead competency.
